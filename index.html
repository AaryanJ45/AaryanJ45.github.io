Topic: The topic of our project is the Analysis of Battery/Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (BEV's and PHEV's) Registrations in Washington State.

Some questions we are trying to answer: Firstly, we want to do some trend analysis, looking at how the registrations of BEVs and PHEVs have changed over time in the state of Washington. We also want to look at what are the most popular models of BEVs and PHEVs in Washington using popularity and diversity statistics. We will also use hypothesis testing to look into relations about certain aspects of the data, for example, looking at if there is a correlation between electric range and base MSRP in these electric vehicles. For machine learning predictions, we look to answer questions about if we can predict the demand of BEVs vs. PHEVs based on demographic and geographic factors, and if there are distinct clusters of counties or regions based on their electric behicle registration patterns.

Why is answering these questions important: Answering these questions are important because firstly, understanding the trends and factors influencing electric vehicle registrations can inform state policies on infrastructure development, like charging stations, and elective vehicle purchase incentives. Analyzing the shift to electirc vehicles can also help quantify the environmental benefits, which is very important for future planning. This data could also be valuable for car manufacturers and stakeholders in the electric car market to understand consumer trends

Data Curation

Source of the data: We got our data from the Data.gov website (LINK). During this phase, we are going to be focusing on importing the data from the website and transforming it so that it's ready for analysis.

In this tutorial, we are going to be using data of Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) and Plug in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) that are currently registered through Wasington State Department of Licensing (DOL). The data includes information about the make, model, year, electric range, base MSRP, and the county. After going to the Data.gov and finding a dataset that best fit our project, we downloaded this dataset as a CSV file. This allows us to easily import the data into our Python environment, and using Pandas, to set up a a data frame in order to manipulate the data.
